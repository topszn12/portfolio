Working With Others

Over the course of the Summer of 2014, I worked with a start-up company that was building an education platform. To my surprise, when I found out that I would be working with this company, I learned that we wouldn’t necessarily be together in once place. If anything, our small development group would be scattered across multiple parts of the world.

This style of working was unfamiliar to me. I didn’t know how I would react to it, but thanks to modern day technology, we didn’t necessarily require to be in one place to work together. The only thing that ever really mattered in this situation was that we all provided equal input.

Our work sessions consisted of daily video calls, weekly sprints, and weekend review sessions. In other words, it incorporated all the elements of regular development, without ever having to physically meet up. If anything, I felt that I accomplished more with a team online than I ever have with a team in person.

The truth of the matter is that this was only possible because everyone on our team played their role. Everyone always contributed what they needed to which enabled us to finish up our project for that summer.

My biggest takeaway from this experience is learning how interdependent teams can be. In other words, you are only as strong as your weakest link. In order for people to succeed as a unit, we must all ensure that we do what is necessary in order to move forward with a specific goal or task. This work style is something I’ve learned to take beyond development and because of it, I’ve become a better worker as well as team member.
