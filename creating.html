Creating

From the time I was young, I always wanted to be an artist. From the moment I knew I could create things with just a pencil or a crayon, I knew that I wanted to practice drawing as much as I could. Developing this hobby became much more to me as I grew up. It taught me that drawing isn’t necessarily just about putting lines on a paper, it’s about bringing ideas to life.

This concept was very difficult to understand for me as kid. I never understood what people meant about gathering your thoughts and conceptualizing them, I just wanted to start drawing. As I got older, it stood out to me that I often struggled coming up with original work. What I mean by that, is that I was so used to imitating the things I saw and calling them my own. I was good at it and many people always praised the things I drew. The thing is, I can’t say that I created Spongebob Squarepants or Batman just because I remade them in a drawing.

It was at that moment where I began to understand just how difficult it is to create something. From then on, I practiced creating my own works every day. They weren’t always good, but they helped me become better at both drawing and coming up with unique ideas.

The thing that was most rewarding for me was that I was able to apply the skills I learned beyond the drawing board. Though I can’t necessarily call myself a full time professional, I can say with confidence that I’m a heck of a web designer, graphic designer, and entrepreneurial enthusiast. Though these mediums aren’t all alike, they are similar in that they require the ability to create.

Whether it be a logo, website, or even a company, coming up with an idea and bringing to life is one of my favorite things to do. I wasn’t a skill I was always great at, but it’s one that I learned to develop and deeply appreciate.
