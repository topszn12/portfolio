Managing

For a short period of time, I did a bit of freelance work. This was something I pursued because I wanted to build up my portfolio and I wanted to make some extra money on the side. It was a very different experience for me because for the first time while working, I didn’t have a boss. I was my own boss.

I struggled immensely at first. I never realize how difficult it would be to manage this endeavor. I was so used to having worked assigned to me, that pursuing clients seemed strange to me. I got better at it as time went by, but it took a lot time and managerial development in order for me to make a good return of investment.

I started by figuring out how much time I had. The good part of being a freelancer is that you get to make your own hours. The downside however, was that I needed to plan it around my own life. It was difficult to balance this type of freedom because I didn’t have anyone to force me to my work other than me. As time went on however, I learned that in order to be successful as a freelancer I needed to be more competent and make better use of my time.

The first change I made, was that I organized the type of work I wanted to pursue. I estimated how long projects would take me and I pursued specific clients based on the type that appealed more to my schedule. Overall this system proved to be effective and allowed me to gain more experience. The second change I made was that I started to take my work time more seriously. It’s easy to get carried away when you’re working at home, so I limited myself in terms of freedom even though I wasn’t in an office setting.

In the end, I ended up walking away from freelancing. Though I enjoyed my time as a freelancer, I realized it takes a lot of personal commitment and that here is the off chance that you may not get payed. That being said, I felt it was a great experience because it taught me a lot about time and personal management skills that I still use today.
